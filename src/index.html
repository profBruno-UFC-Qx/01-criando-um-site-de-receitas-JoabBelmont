Fettucini Alfredo

This authentic recipe is simple but very delicious, some people add too many ingredients to this, but, in this case, less is more.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb fettuccine noddles, dry
  • 12 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 6 oz Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, freshly grated
  • ½ - 1 tbsp salt (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Fill a large pot with water and heat it over high heat until boiling. Once the water boils, add a good dash of salt and the noodles. Cook only until al dente, 9-10 minutes.
  2. While the pasta heats, gently melt the butter over medium heat in a large, non-stick skillet with high sides.
  3. Drain the pasta (reserving the water) and transfer the pasta to the skillet with the melted butter.
  4. Add a ½ tbsp salt and toss the pasta with a tongs to combine (nonstick, silicone tongs work best).
  5. Reduce the heat to medium low. Add a handful of grated cheese and a ladle of hot pasta water, tossing the pasta continuously with your tongs to incorporate the cheese.
  6. Add more cheese, a little at a time, tossing between each addition, until all the cheese has been used.
  7. Add more hot pasta water as needed to melt the cheese so that it coats the noodles (But, be careful not to add too much. You don't want a watery sauce).
  8. Taste the pasta and adjust the salt as necessary.
  9. Serve immediately.

Notes

It is important to use a good quality cheese for this recipe. The cheese is the star of the show, so it needs to be good! Also, be sure to use a good quality pasta. The pasta and cheese are the only ingredients, so they need to be good. You can add some freshly ground black pepper at the end as well.
